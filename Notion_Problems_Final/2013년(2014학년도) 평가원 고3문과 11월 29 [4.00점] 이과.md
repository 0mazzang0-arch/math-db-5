## 문제
함수 $f(x) = 3x^2 - ax$가
$$\lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{1}{n} \sum_{k=1}^{n} f \left( \frac{3k}{n} \right) = f(1)$$
을 만족시킬 때, 상수 $a$의 값을 구하시오.

## 보기

## 풀이
$$\lim_{n \to \infty} \frac{1}{n} \sum_{k=1}^{n} f \left( \frac{3k}{n} \right)$$
$$= \frac{1}{3} \times \lim_{n \to \infty} \sum_{k=1}^{n} f \left( \frac{3k}{n} \right) \cdot \frac{3}{n}$$
$$= \frac{1}{3} \times \int_{0}^{3} f(x) dx$$
$$= \frac{1}{3} \times \int_{0}^{3} (3x^2 - ax) dx$$
$$= \frac{1}{3} \times \left[ x^3 - \frac{a}{2}x^2 \right]_{0}^{3}$$
$$= \frac{1}{3} \times \left( 27 - \frac{9}{2}a \right)$$
$$= 9 - \frac{3a}{2}$$

주어진 조건에 의해
$9 - \frac{3a}{2} = f(1)$
$9 - \frac{3a}{2} = 3 - a$
$\frac{a}{2} = 6$
$\therefore a = 12$

[정답] $12$
