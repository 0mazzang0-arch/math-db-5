## 문제
$\lim_{x \to \infty} (\sqrt{x^{2}+ax+1}-x+b)=1$ 일 때, $a+2b$ 의 값은?

## 보기
① $1$   ② $2$   ③ $3$   ④ $4$   ⑤ $5$

## 풀이
$\lim_{x \to \infty} (\sqrt{x^{2}+ax+1}-x+b)$
$= \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{x^{2}+ax+1-(x^{2}-2bx+b^{2})}{\sqrt{x^{2}+ax+1}+(x-b)}$
$= \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{(a+2b)x+(1-b^{2})}{\sqrt{x^{2}+ax+1}+x-b}$
$= \lim_{x \to \infty} \frac{a+2b+\frac{1-b^{2}}{x}}{\sqrt{1+\frac{a}{x}+\frac{1}{x^{2}}}+1-\frac{b}{x}}$
$= \frac{a+2b}{2}=1$
따라서, $a+2b=2$
