## 문제
$\sin \alpha = \frac{1}{3}$ 일 때, $\cos \left( \frac{\pi}{3} + \alpha \right)$ 의 값은? (단, $0 < \alpha < \frac{\pi}{2}$)

## 보기
① $\frac{2 \sqrt{2} - \sqrt{3}}{6}$   ② $\frac{2 - \sqrt{3}}{6}$   ③ $\frac{\sqrt{2} - 1}{3}$   ④ $\frac{\sqrt{3} - \sqrt{2}}{3}$   ⑤ $\frac{\sqrt{3} - 1}{3}$

## 풀이
$\sin \alpha = \frac{1}{3}$ 이므로 
$\cos \alpha = \sqrt{1 - \left( \frac{1}{3} \right)^{2}} = \frac{2 \sqrt{2}}{3}$ (단, $0 < \alpha < \frac{\pi}{2}$)

따라서 $\cos \left( \frac{\pi}{3} + \alpha \right) = \cos \frac{\pi}{3} \cdot \cos \alpha - \sin \frac{\pi}{3} \cdot \sin \alpha$
$= \frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{2 \sqrt{2}}{3} - \frac{\sqrt{3}}{2} \cdot \frac{1}{3}$
$= \frac{2 \sqrt{2} - \sqrt{3}}{6}$

정답: ①
