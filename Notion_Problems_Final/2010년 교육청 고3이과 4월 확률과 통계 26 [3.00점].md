## 문제
$\cos \alpha = -\frac{1}{3}$, $\sin \beta = \frac{\sqrt{2}}{4}$일 때, $\cos (\alpha - \beta)$의 값은? (단, $\frac{\pi}{2} \leq \alpha \leq \pi$, $0 \leq \beta \leq \frac{\pi}{2}$)

## 보기
① $\frac{3 - \sqrt{14}}{12}$ ② $\frac{-4 + \sqrt{14}}{12}$ ③ $\frac{4 - \sqrt{14}}{12}$ ④ $\frac{-3 + \sqrt{14}}{12}$ ⑤ $\frac{3 + \sqrt{14}}{12}$

## 풀이
$\sin \alpha = \sqrt{1 - \cos^{2} \alpha} = \frac{2 \sqrt{2}}{3}$, $\cos \beta = \sqrt{1 - \sin^{2} \beta} = \frac{\sqrt{14}}{4}$이므로
$\cos (\alpha - \beta) = \cos \alpha \cos \beta + \sin \alpha \sin \beta$
$= \left( -\frac{1}{3} \right) \cdot \frac{\sqrt{14}}{4} + \left( \frac{2 \sqrt{2}}{3} \right) \cdot \frac{\sqrt{2}}{4}$
$= -\frac{\sqrt{14}}{12} + \frac{4}{12} = \frac{4 - \sqrt{14}}{12}$

정답: ③
