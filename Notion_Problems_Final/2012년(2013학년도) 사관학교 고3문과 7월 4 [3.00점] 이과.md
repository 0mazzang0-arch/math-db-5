## 문제
$\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{2 \left( \frac{1}{2} + x \right)^{4} - 2 \left( \frac{1}{2} \right)^{4}}{x}$ 의 값은?

## 보기
① $\frac{1}{2}$   ② $\frac{2}{3}$   ③ $\frac{3}{4}$   ④ $1$   ⑤ $\frac{3}{2}$

## 풀이
[정답] ④

$f(x) = 2 \left( \frac{1}{2} + x \right)^{4}$ 이라 하면 $f(0) = 2 \left( \frac{1}{2} \right)^{4}$ 이므로
$\lim_{x \to 0} \frac{2 \left( \frac{1}{2} + x \right)^{4} - 2 \left( \frac{1}{2} \right)^{4}}{x} = f'(0)$

$f'(x) = 8 \left( \frac{1}{2} + x \right)^{3}$ 이므로
$f'(0) = 1$

[다른풀이]
$f(x) = 2 \left( \frac{1}{2} + x \right)^{4}$ 이라 놓으면
$f'(x) = 8 \left( \frac{1}{2} + x \right)^{3}$

$\therefore \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{2 \left( \frac{1}{2} + x \right)^{4} - 2 \left( \frac{1}{2} \right)^{4}}{x} = \lim_{x \to 0} \frac{f(x) - f(0)}{x}$
$= f'(0) = 8 \cdot \left( \frac{1}{2} \right)^{3} = 1$
